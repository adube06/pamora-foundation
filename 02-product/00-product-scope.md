# Product Scope

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This document defines the boundaries of the Pamora platform.

It answers three questions:

1. What is Pamora?
2. What is included?
3. What is intentionally excluded?

A clearly defined scope prevents feature creep and protects long-term product focus.

---

# Product Definition

Pamora is an **Occasion Collaboration Platform** with an integrated Vendor Marketplace.

It enables people to plan, coordinate, fund, execute, and preserve important occasions through one shared digital workspace.

The product is built around the complete lifecycle of an Occasion rather than isolated event-related features.

---

# Core Objective

Pamora helps users move from:

> **"We have an occasion to organize."**

to

> **"The occasion was successfully completed."**

Everything within the product should support this transformation.

# In Scope

The following capabilities are part of the Pamora platform.

---

# 1. Occasion Management

Users can:

* Create occasions.
* Configure occasion details.
* Manage lifecycle stages.
* Archive completed occasions.
* Duplicate previous occasions as templates (future).

---

# 2. Participant Management

Support for:

* Hosts
* Committee Members
* Guests
* Family Members
* Vendors
* Organization Members

Participants interact within the context of an Occasion.

---

# 3. Invitations

* Digital invitations
* Public invitation pages
* QR codes
* RSVP
* Guest lists
* Reminder messages

---

# 4. Committee Collaboration

Committee members can:

* Receive responsibilities.
* Track assigned work.
* Share updates.
* Communicate.

---

# 5. Planning

# 6. Finance

# Out of Scope (Current)

Pamora is **not** intended to become:

## Ticketing Platform

Examples:

* Concert ticket sales
* Stadium access
* Event admission management

---

## Conference Registration Platform

Examples:

* Badge printing
* Session registration
* Exhibition management

---

## Social Network

Pamora does not exist for continuous social interaction unrelated to an Occasion.

---

## General Accounting Software

Pamora tracks Occasion finances.

It is not intended to replace accounting systems.

---

## ERP

Pamora is not an enterprise resource planning platform.

---

## Messaging Platform

Messaging supports collaboration but is not intended to replace dedicated communication applications.

---

# Product Boundaries

Every proposed feature should strengthen one of the following business domains:

* Occasion
* Planning
* Finance
* Marketplace
* Communication
* Media
* Identity

If a feature cannot be mapped to an existing domain, it requires strategic review before approval.

---

# Scope Expansion Rules

The platform should expand only when:

* Customer demand is validated.
* Existing workflows remain simple.
* Engineering complexity is manageable.
* Business value is measurable.

Expansion should be evolutionary rather than revolutionary.

---

# Product Success

Pamora succeeds when users can:

* Organize efficiently.
* Collaborate effectively.
* Track finances confidently.
* Coordinate vendors easily.
* Preserve meaningful memories.

The product should always prioritize successful Occasion outcomes over feature quantity.
