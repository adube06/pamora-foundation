# Purpose

This document defines **why Pamora exists today** and how the company fulfills its vision.

While the Vision describes where Pamora is going, the Mission defines what Pamora does every day to move toward that future.

Every feature, engineering decision, customer interaction, and business initiative should support this mission.

---

# Mission Statement

> **To empower individuals, families, communities, organizations, and businesses to successfully plan, coordinate, fund, and execute important occasions through one trusted digital platform.**

Pamora exists to reduce stress, improve collaboration, increase transparency, and help organizers deliver successful occasions.

---

# Our Responsibility

Pamora is responsible for simplifying the operational side of occasion management.

We are not responsible for creating the occasion itself.

We provide the infrastructure that enables people to organize it successfully.

---

# The Customer Problem

Organizers face recurring challenges:

* Information is scattered across multiple apps.
* Responsibilities are unclear.
* Financial contributions are difficult to track.
* Vendors are managed manually.
* Deadlines are missed.
* Communication becomes fragmented.
* Planning knowledge disappears after the occasion.

Pamora solves these problems by bringing planning, collaboration, communication, and finance into a single workspace.

---

# Who We Serve

Pamora is built for people organizing meaningful occasions.

Primary customer groups include:

* Families
* Wedding committees
* Funeral committees
* Community groups
* Religious organizations
* NGOs
* Schools
* Businesses
* Event service providers

---

# What We Deliver

Pamora delivers value through:

## Planning

Helping organizers transform ideas into structured execution plans.

## Coordination

Connecting hosts, committee members, guests, and vendors within one platform.

## Transparency

Providing clear visibility into budgets, contributions, responsibilities, and progress.

## Accountability

Ensuring every important action can be tracked and audited where appropriate.

## Confidence

Reducing uncertainty by giving organizers clear visibility into what has been completed and what still requires attention.

---

# How We Measure Success

Pamora does not measure success only by technical metrics.

Meaningful indicators include:

* Successful occasions completed
* Active collaboration among participants
* Reduction in planning errors
* Increased financial transparency
* Positive organizer satisfaction
* Vendor trust and repeat usage

---

# Engineering Alignment

Every engineering decision should support at least one of the following goals:

* Reduce organizer effort.
* Improve collaboration.
* Increase trust.
* Improve platform reliability.
* Simplify user experience.
* Protect business data.
* Enable long-term scalability.

If an implementation does not contribute to these goals, it should be questioned.

---

# Product Alignment

Every new feature must answer these questions before approval:

1. Which customer problem does it solve?
2. Which user benefits?
3. Which business domain owns it?
4. How does it improve the occasion lifecycle?
5. How will success be measured?

Features without a clear answer should not enter development.

---

# Mission Principles

Pamora believes that:

* Technology should simplify people's lives.
* Trust is earned through consistency.
* Collaboration creates better outcomes.
* Transparency reduces conflict.
* Great software should feel invisible while helping people accomplish meaningful work.
