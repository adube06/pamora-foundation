# Purpose

This document defines the single most important metric that guides the growth of Pamora.

A North Star Metric aligns product, engineering, design, operations, and business teams around one measurable outcome that reflects long-term customer value.

Pamora should optimize for meaningful outcomes rather than vanity metrics.

---

# North Star Metric

## Successful Occasions Completed

A **Successful Occasion** is an Occasion that reaches completion while using Pamora as the primary coordination platform.

Completion is measured by the successful execution of the occasion, not simply by its creation.

---

# Why This Metric?

Many software products measure:

* Downloads
* Registered users
* Active users
* Revenue
* Page views

These metrics are useful but do not prove that customers achieved their goal.

Pamora exists to help people successfully organize important occasions.

Therefore, the product should optimize for completed occasions rather than application activity.

# Success Formula

Pamora creates value when the following cycle is completed:

Create Occasion

↓

Invite Participants

↓

Plan Together

↓

Coordinate Activities

↓

Manage Contributions

↓

Book Vendors

↓

Execute Occasion

↓

Preserve Memories

↓

Return for the Next Occasion

Every improvement to the platform should strengthen this lifecycle.

---

# Decision Filter

Before starting any feature, ask:

* Will this help users complete occasions more successfully?
* Will this increase trust?
* Will this reduce organizer effort?
* Will this improve collaboration?
* Will this encourage repeat usage?

If the answer is "no", reconsider the feature.

---

# Long-Term Objective

Pamora's long-term objective is to become the trusted digital infrastructure behind millions of successful occasions across Africa.

Growth is measured by customer success, not by software complexity.
