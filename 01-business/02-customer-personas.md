# Customer Personas

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This document defines the primary customer groups served by Pamora.

Pamora is a multi-sided platform. Different users join the platform with different goals, responsibilities, and expectations.

Every product decision should identify which persona it primarily serves.

---

# Platform Model

Pamora is not built for a single type of user.

It serves an ecosystem where multiple participants interact around the same Occasion.

The primary personas are:

1. Occasion Host
2. Committee Member
3. Guest
4. Vendor
5. Organization

Each persona creates value for the others.

---

# Persona 1 — Occasion Host

## Description

The Occasion Host is the person responsible for creating and managing an Occasion.

The Host is accountable for ensuring that the Occasion is successfully completed.

Examples:

* Bride or Groom
* Family Representative
* Funeral Organizer
* Birthday Organizer
* Corporate Event Coordinator

---

## Goals

* Create an Occasion.
* Organize planning.
* Invite participants.
* Coordinate committee members.
* Track contributions.
* Manage expenses.
* Hire vendors.
* Monitor progress.

---

## Frustrations

* Too many WhatsApp messages.
* Lost planning information.
* Unclear responsibilities.
* Budget uncertainty.
* Vendor coordination.
* Last-minute surprises.

---

## Success Criteria

The Host should always know:

* What has been completed.
* What is pending.
* Who is responsible.
* Financial status.
* Event readiness.

---

# Persona 2 — Committee Member

## Description

Committee Members assist the Host in organizing the Occasion.

Their responsibilities differ depending on the committee structure.

Examples:

* Treasurer
* Secretary
* Chairperson
* Logistics Coordinator
* Catering Coordinator

---

## Goals

* Complete assigned work.
* Collaborate with others.
* Update progress.
* Communicate effectively.
* Access relevant information.

---

## Pain Points

* Responsibilities are unclear.
* Information is scattered.
* Decisions happen privately.
* Difficult to track progress.

---

## Success Criteria

Committee Members should clearly understand:

* Their responsibilities.
* Upcoming deadlines.
* Assigned tasks.
* Relevant discussions.

---

# Persona 3 — Guest

## Description

Guests participate in the Occasion but are not responsible for organizing it.

Guests may contribute financially, RSVP, receive updates, and interact with the Occasion.

---

## Goals

* View invitation.
* Confirm attendance.
* Find venue information.
* Contribute easily.
* Receive updates.
* Celebrate the Occasion.

---

## Pain Points

* Missing information.
* Forgotten dates.
* Confusing contribution instructions.
* Multiple communication channels.

---

## Success Criteria

Guests should always know:

* Where the Occasion is.
* When it happens.
* What is expected.
* How to contribute.
* Latest announcements.

---

# Persona 4 — Vendor

## Description

Vendors provide products or services through the Pamora Marketplace.

Examples include:

* Photographer
* Decorator
* Caterer
* DJ
* MC
* Tent Rental
* Sound Equipment
* Car Hire
* Makeup Artist

---

## Goals

* Receive bookings.
* Manage availability.
* Showcase services.
* Build reputation.
* Increase income.

---

## Pain Points

* Manual booking process.
* Double bookings.
* Last-minute cancellations.
* Limited online visibility.
* Unverified customers.

---

## Success Criteria

Vendors should be able to:

* Manage bookings confidently.
* Update availability.
* Track completed work.
* Build trusted reviews.
* Grow repeat business.

---

# Persona 5 — Organization

## Description

Organizations manage recurring occasions involving multiple people.

Examples:

* Churches
* Mosques
* NGOs
* Schools
* Universities
* Companies
* Community Associations

---

## Goals

* Standardize planning.
* Coordinate teams.
* Maintain historical records.
* Improve accountability.
* Reuse planning templates.

---

## Pain Points

* Repeating planning work.
* Poor record keeping.
* Lack of transparency.
* Difficult reporting.

---

## Success Criteria

Organizations should be able to run multiple occasions with consistent processes and clear oversight.

---

# Secondary Personas

These personas are not primary decision-makers but interact with the platform.

* Family Members
* Sponsors
* Donors
* Volunteers
* Speakers
* Performers
* Government Representatives
* Religious Leaders
* Security Personnel

Future releases may introduce dedicated experiences for some of these personas.

---

# Persona Priority (MVP)

The MVP should optimize for the following order:

1. Occasion Host
2. Committee Member
3. Guest
4. Vendor

Organizational capabilities should be introduced after the core consumer workflow is validated.

---

# Design Principles

Every feature should identify:

* Primary Persona
* Secondary Persona
* Business Value
* Success Metric

Avoid building features without a clearly defined customer.
