# Purpose

This document defines how Pamora creates, delivers, captures, and compounds value.

It serves as the commercial blueprint of the platform and guides long-term strategic decisions.

---

# Executive Summary

Pamora is a **multi-sided platform**.

It connects four primary participants:

* Occasion Hosts
* Committees
* Guests
* Vendors

Each participant increases the value of the platform for the others.

The more occasions created, the more vendors receive bookings.

The more vendors join, the more attractive the platform becomes for organizers.

This creates a self-reinforcing ecosystem.

---

# Platform Position

Pamora is **not**:

* A digital invitation app.
* A fundraising app.
* A vendor directory.
* A task management app.

Pamora combines these capabilities into a single operating system centered around an Occasion.

Every feature strengthens the overall ecosystem rather than existing independently.

# Strategic Principles

Pamora should prioritize:

* Trust over rapid monetization.
* Retention over acquisition.
* Ecosystem growth over isolated features.
* Long-term relationships over one-time transactions.

---

# Business Risks

Potential risks include:

* Marketplace liquidity during early stages.
* Slow vendor adoption.
* Dependence on payment integrations.
* Maintaining platform simplicity as features grow.

These risks should be managed through phased rollout and continuous customer validation.

---

# Definition of Success

Pamora succeeds when:

* Organizers return for future occasions.
* Vendors depend on Pamora for business.
* Guests trust the platform.
* Communities recommend Pamora organically.
* The ecosystem grows without relying solely on paid marketing.

---

# Strategic Observation — Two Engines Inside One Product

After analyzing Pamora in depth, one important conclusion emerges:

**Pamora contains two businesses inside one product.**

1. **Occasion OS (Operating System)** — planning, committees, budgets, invitations, timelines, communication.
2. **Marketplace** — vendors, bookings, services, rentals.

These are two distinct growth engines.

Therefore, in the PRD and architecture, they should be separated as distinct Business Domains that collaborate through the Occasion as the core aggregate. This keeps the system easy to extend and easy to maintain even as it scales.
