# Revenue Model

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Pamora generates sustainable revenue while preserving user trust and long-term platform growth.

Revenue should always be aligned with customer value.

Pamora should monetize outcomes, not access.

---

# Revenue Philosophy

Pamora is a trust-based platform.

People use Pamora during some of the most important moments of their lives.

Revenue decisions must never create the perception that Pamora is exploiting weddings, funerals, or community fundraising.

Trust is more valuable than short-term income.

---

# Monetization Strategy

Pamora follows a phased monetization strategy.

## Phase 1 — Build Trust

Primary Goal:

Grow the ecosystem.

Hosts should be able to create and manage occasions without paying.

Guests should never be charged simply for participating.

The objective is adoption, not revenue.

---

## Phase 2 — Marketplace Revenue

Once the marketplace reaches healthy activity, Pamora earns revenue from successful vendor transactions.

Possible models:

* Commission per completed booking.
* Fixed platform service fee.
* Hybrid commission model.

Revenue is generated because Pamora created business for the vendor.

---

## Phase 3 — Vendor Subscription

Professional vendors may subscribe to premium tools.

Potential benefits include:

* Featured placement
* Business analytics
* Promotional campaigns
* Additional team members
* Branded storefront
* Advanced reporting
* Priority customer support

Core marketplace participation should remain free.

---

## Phase 4 — Organization Plans

Organizations with recurring occasions may subscribe for:

* Multiple workspaces
* Administrative controls
* Reporting dashboards
* Approval workflows
* Audit logs
* Custom branding

This creates predictable recurring revenue.

---

## Phase 5 — Platform Services

As the ecosystem matures, Pamora may introduce optional premium services such as:

* Payment processing
* AI planning assistant
* Vendor financing
* Event insurance partnerships
* Logistics integrations
* API access
* Data insights for enterprise customers

These services should enhance the platform without becoming mandatory.

---

# Revenue Sources

## Marketplace Commission

Source:

Vendor bookings.

Primary payer:

Vendor.

Reason:

Pamora generated qualified business opportunities.

---

## Premium Vendor Subscription

Source:

Monthly or annual subscription.

Primary payer:

Vendor.

Reason:

Business growth tools.

---

## Organization Subscription

Source:

Recurring subscription.

Primary payer:

Organizations.

Reason:

Operational efficiency.

---

## Payment Processing

Source:

Transaction processing fees where permitted.

Primary payer:

Depends on payment flow and regulatory requirements.

Reason:

Convenience and automation.

---

## Advertising (Limited)

Pamora should avoid intrusive advertising.

Only contextually relevant promotions may be considered.

Examples:

* Featured vendors.
* Sponsored services.
* Seasonal campaigns.

User experience should always come first.

---

# Revenue Principles

Pamora will **not** charge for:

* Creating an account.
* Viewing invitations.
* Receiving invitations.
* RSVP responses.
* Basic occasion creation.
* Basic collaboration.

These activities are essential to ecosystem growth.

---

# Pricing Philosophy

Pricing should be:

* Transparent.
* Predictable.
* Easy to understand.
* Justified by delivered value.

Hidden fees should never exist.

---

# Customer Lifetime Value

Pamora aims to increase customer lifetime value through repeated usage.

Examples:

* Wedding today.
* Birthday next year.
* Graduation later.
* Community fundraiser.
* Corporate event.

The same customer should naturally return to the platform.

Retention is more valuable than one-time monetization.

---

# Strategic Revenue Goals

Revenue should grow because:

* More occasions are successfully completed.
* More vendors join.
* More organizations adopt Pamora.
* More premium services are used.

Revenue should never depend on increasing friction for users.

---

# Risks

Potential risks include:

* Charging too early and slowing adoption.
* Over-monetizing sensitive occasions.
* Vendor dissatisfaction with commission levels.
* Regulatory changes affecting payment processing.

These risks should be reviewed regularly.

---

# Success Indicators

The revenue model is successful when:

* Vendors earn measurable business value.
* Hosts continue returning without financial barriers.
* Organizations adopt paid plans voluntarily.
* Revenue grows alongside ecosystem value.
* Customer trust remains high.

---

# Architecture Decision Candidate — Hosts Are Not the Primary Revenue Source

After analyzing Pamora, one decision should be formalized:

**Hosts should never be the primary source of revenue.**

Instead:

* Hosts → Generate demand.
* Guests → Grow the network.
* Vendors → Gain business.
* Organizations → Gain efficiency.

Therefore, Pamora's revenue should come primarily from:

* Vendors
* Organizations
* Premium platform services

The one exception: when a Host is preparing an occasion, they may pay for invitation cards, entrance management, or both — commonly delivered through a digital e-card, which is how most people already do this.
